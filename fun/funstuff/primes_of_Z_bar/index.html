Prime Ideals of $\overline{\mathbb Z}$

We wish to examine the prime ideals of the integral completion of \(\mathbb Z\) in the algebraic closure of \(\mathbb Q\). We denote this ring as $\overline {\mathbb Z}$. First, we let \(\mathscr L\) denote the collection of all finite extensions \(L\) over \(\mathbb Q\). For each \(L \in \mathscr L\), we choose an embedding \(f_L:L\hookrightarrow \overline {\mathbb Q}\). For every \(K \in \mathscr L\), and a finite extension of \(K\) called \(L\in \mathcal L\), there is a unique embedding \(f_K^L:K\hookrightarrow L\) such that \(f_K = f_L \circ f_K^L\). This is equivalent to the following diagram commuting: $$\begin{CD} \overline{\mathbb Q} @= \overline{\mathbb Q} \\ @A f_L AA @A f_K AA \\ L @< f_K^L<< K \end{CD}$$ Now we can begin studying the prime ideals of \(\overline{\mathbb Z}\). Suppose that \(\mathscr P = \{\mathfrak p_L\}_{L\in \mathscr L}\) is a collection of prime ideals \(\mathfrak p_L \subset \mathcal O_L\) with the following property:
For any \(L, K \in \mathscr L\) such that \(L\) is a finite extension of \(K\), then \(\left(f_K^L\right)^{-1}(\mathfrak p_L) = \mathfrak p_K\).
In other words: \(\mathfrak p_L | f_K^L(\mathfrak p_K) \). Such a collection will henceforth be called a well-formed collection of prime ideals of \(\mathscr L\).

Lemma 1: If \(\mathscr P\) is a well-formed collection of prime ideals of \(\mathscr L\), then \[\mathfrak p := \bigcup_{\mathfrak p_L \in \mathscr P} f_L(\mathfrak p_L) \subset \overline{\mathbb Z}\] is a prime ideal of \(\overline{\mathbb Z}\).

Lemma 2: Let $\mathscr P$ be the a well-formed collections of prime ideals of $\mathscr L$. If there exists some $\mathfrak p_L \in \mathscr P$ such that $\mathfrak p_L = 0$, then for all $\mathfrak p_K \in \mathscr P$, $\mathfrak p_K = 0$.

Proof of Lemma 2

Suppose that $\mathfrak p_L = 0$. Since $\mathscr P$ is well-formed, we know that $\mathfrak p_{\mathbb Q} = (f_{\mathbb Q}^L)^{-1}(0)$. Take any $K\in \mathscr L$. Then $(f_\mathbb{Q}^K)^{-1}(\mathfrak p_K) = 0$. Since $N_K(x) \in \mathfrak p_K$ for any $x \in \mathfrak p_K$, it must be that $N_K(x) = 0$ for all $x \in \mathfrak p_K$. Therefore, $x=0$ and $\mathfrak p_K = 0$.

Therefore, we can define the (unique) well-formed collection of primes ideals containing $0$ to be the $0$ collection.
Moreover, given a prime ideal $\mathfrak p \subset \overline{\mathbb Z}$, we can create a well-formed collection of prime ideals $\mathscr P = \{ f_L^{-1}(\mathfrak p)\}_{L\in \mathscr L}$. This is well-formed almost definitionally. We say $\mathscr P$ is induced by $\mathfrak p$.

Lemma 3: Let $\mathscr P$ be the well-formed collection of prime ideals of $\mathscr L$ induced by the prime ideal $\mathfrak p \subset \overline{\mathbb Z}$. Then $$ \mathfrak p = \bigcup_{\mathfrak p_L \in \mathscr P} f_L(\mathfrak p_L) = \bigcup_{L\in \mathscr L} f_L(f_L^{-1} (\mathfrak p))$$

Proof of Lemma 3

By set theory, we immediately have $$ \mathfrak p \supset \bigcup_{L\in \mathscr L} f_L(f_L^{-1} (\mathfrak p))$$ Take arbitrary $x\in \mathfrak p$. We aim to show that $x \in f_L(f_L^{-1}(\mathfrak p))$ for some $L\in \mathscr L$.
We know that there exists $K \in \mathscr L$ such that $x\in f_K(\mathcal O_K)$. Since $f_K$ is injective, it is an isomorphism from $\mathcal O_K$ to $f_K(\mathcal O_K)$. Therefore, there exists a unique $y \in \mathcal O_K$ such that $f_K(y)=x$. It follows that $y \in f_K^{-1}(\mathfrak p)$ and therefore $x \in f_K(f_K^{-1}(\mathfrak p))$. Therefore $$ \mathfrak p = \bigcup_{L\in \mathscr L} f_L(f_L^{-1} (\mathfrak p))$$

Lemma 4: Let $\mathscr P$ be a well-formed collection of primes ideals of $\mathscr L$. If we define the prime idea $$\mathfrak p := \bigcup_{\mathfrak p_L \in \mathscr P} f_L(\mathfrak p_L) \subset \overline{\mathbb Z}$$ and define $\mathscr P'$ to be the well-formed collection of prime ideals of $\mathscr L$ induced by $\mathfrak p$, then $\mathscr P =\mathscr P'$.

Proof of Lemma 4

We need to show that for all $L\in \mathscr L$, $\mathfrak p_L = \mathfrak p'_L$. We know that $\mathfrak p'_L = f_L^{-1}(\mathfrak p)$. By set theory, we see that $\mathfrak p_L \subset f_L^{-1}(\mathfrak p)$. There are two cases. Case 1: $\mathfrak p_L = 0$. Then by Lemma 2, $\mathscr P = 0$, so $\mathfrak p = 0$. Therefore, $f_L^{-1}(\mathfrak p) = 0$, so $\mathscr P' = 0$. Case 2: $\mathfrak p_L \neq 0$, so $\mathfrak p_L$ is maximal, since every nonzero prime ideal is maximal in $\mathcal O_L$. Since $f_L^{-1}(\mathfrak p)$ is a proper ideal of $\mathcal O_L$, we see that $\mathfrak p_L = f_L^{-1}(\mathfrak p)$. Therefore, $\mathscr P = \mathscr P'$.

Theorem 5: Prime ideals of $\overline{\mathbb Z}$ are in one to one correspondence with well-formed collections of prime ideals of $\mathscr L$.
